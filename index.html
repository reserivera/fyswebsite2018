Filipino Youth Summit

  • What is FYS?

    The Filipino Youth Summit (FYS) is an advocacy-based youth conference in the country where student-leaders gather to participate in reviewing local and national government initiatives, addressing national issues, and collaborating towards a more empowered and progressive nation. The summit will be composed of collaborative discussions, and issues-centered talks to further unite the Filipino Youth of common interest.

  • Vision Mission

    Through greater awareness and understanding among the regions, FYS 2018 envisions to provide a platform for youth leaders from all over the country to share ideas for social transformation.

  • Objectives

    > To promote a deeper understanding of various national issues

    > To elevate the involvement of the Filipino Youth at national and regional activities

    > To build a network of youth committed to developing long-term solutions

  • The Organizers

    The Filipino Youth Summit DLSU is brought to you by the Office of the Vice President for External Affairs (OVPEA). Click here to see the organizers.

  • The Central Committee

    The Central Committee of the Filipino Youth Summit comprises of students from De La Salle University. It is composed of twelve (12) different committees: Programs, External Programs, Facilitators, Delegate Relations, Corporate Relations, Media & Publicity, Digital Content, Secretariat, Finance, Materials, Food, and Venue. Click here to see the list of team leaders.

  • The Filipino Youth Leaders

    The Filipino Youth Leaders serve as the representatives from the different regions in the Philippines. In the conference, they will be assisting the Central Committee in ensuring the success of the summit. Click here to see the list of the youth leaders.

Academic Tools

  1. Pillars and Topics

    1. Business Ethics

      2. The Business Ethics Pillar is centered on promoting and conforming to ethical business standards in order for future business leaders to remain professional, ethical, and responsible towards a more progressive society. This pillar aims to create a healthy discussion and proper discourse in the realms of business ethics in order to raise awareness for the country’s biggest issues.

      1. The Philippine Labor and Employment Plan 2017-2022
      2. Department of Labor in OFW IDs
      3. Bayanihan Economy of Bam Aquino

    2. Education

      3. The Education Pillar is centered on initiatives to ensure the quality and accessibility of education to each Filipino citizen, as well as the socio-economic empowerment of the Filipino teachers. This pillar aims to analyze the weaknesses in our educational system, and bridge these gaps through progressive solutions.

      1. Department of Budget (DBM) Plan to raise teachers wages in 2020
      2. Department of Education's Order No. 32, s. 2015 on Indigenous Peoples Education Curriculum Framework
      3. Department of Education's Accreditation and Equivalency Program (ALS) for out-of-school youth

    3. Environment

      4. The Environment Pillar is centered on advocating a sustainable tourism industry. This pillar aims to encourage a sense of responsibility in stimulating the local tourism industry through promoting environmental protection among tourists and sustainable business practices among business owners.

      1. Boracay Closure Project
      2. Ecological Solid Waste Management Act of 2000 Republic Act No. 9003
      3. Philippine Environment Code/Presidential Decree No. 1152

    4. Human Rights

      5. The Human Rights Pillar is centered on initiatives that promote the advancement of the rights of Filipino citizens, specifically underrepresented minorities. This pillar aims to emphasize the importance of human rights in all its forms.

      1. 5-year human rights plan of the Duterte-Cayetano Administration
      2. Philippine Framework Plan for Women's action plan on Gender-Responsive Governance
      3. UN Standard Minimum Rules for the treatment of Prisoners in correlation to the Supreme Court's Initiatives to prevent the overcrowding of Jails

    5. Public Health

      6. The Public Health Pillar is centered on initiatives related to health, specifically government's financial support and goals in health issues which caters to the marginalized. This pillar aims to raise awareness and review health programs of the government units.

      1. Department of Health (DOH)'s Mental Health Program
      2. Kalusugan Pangkalahatan
      3. National Health Insurance Program

GENERAL

Frequently Asked Questions

  • WHAT IS FYS?

    1. The Filipino Youth Summit (FYS) is an advocacy-based youth conference in the country where student-leaders gather to participate in reviewing local and national government initiatives, addressing national issues, and collaborating towards a more empowered and progressive nation. The summit will be composed of collaborative discussions, and issues-centered talks to further unite the Filipino Youth of common interest.

  • WHO ARE ELIGIBLE TO BECOME FYS DELEGATE?

    1. Any individual who meets the following criteria is eligible to become a delegate of the Filipino Youth Summit:
      1. A Filipino Citizen
      2. Youth Aged 15 to 30 years old
      3. Has exemplified excellence on both academics and co-curricular activities

    2. Hence, applications are open to senior high school, undergraduate, and graduate students.


  • WHEN AND WHERE WILL THE CONFERENCE BE?

    1. The official venue of the Filipino Youth Summit is at De La Salle University-Manila, and will be held on August 2-5, 2018.

  • WHAT IS DIFFERENCE BETWEEN A FYS DELEDATE AND A FYS YOUTH LEADER?

    1. FYS Youth Leaders are assisting the organizing team in getting delegates to apply and also are the representatives of certain regions in order to disseminate information across the whole country, while FYS Delegates are the participants of the conference.

    APPLICATION PROCESS

    Frequently Asked Questions

  • HOW DO I APPLY AS A DELEGATE?

    • STEP 1: Fill up the application form at: bit.ly/FYSDLSU-DelegatesApp.
    STEP 2: Release of results will be sent via email.
    STEP 3: Once accepted, confirm your slot as a pioneering delegate.
    STEP 4: Prepare for your assigned pillar.



  • CAN I APPLY EVEN IF I'M FROM A DIFFERENT COUNTRY?

    • Yes, you can apply as an observer. For applications, please fill up the application form at bit.ly/FYSDLSU-DelegatesApp.



  • DO YOU ACCEPT APPLICATIONS AS DELEGATION?

    • Yes, same process will apply when it comes to registering as a delegation. However, the minimum requirement for a delegation is at least five (5) delegates. Faculty advisers may opt to be an observer. He/she is likewise expected to pay the delegate fee.



  • HOW DO I CONFIRM MY SLOT AS A DELEGATE?

    • Please follow the instructions, and deadlines stated in your acceptance letter re: confirmation process. This was sent in the email address you used upon application.



    PAYMENT

    Frequently Asked Questions

  • HOW MUCH IS THE DELEGATION FEE?

    • The delegate fee for both registered Delegates and Observers is P1500.
    Note: International Observers have a different delegate fee.



  • WHAT IS INCLUSIVE IN THE ENTRANCE/ DELEGATE FEE?

    • Inclusive of the delegate’s fee are lunch and snacks for the 4-day conference, exposure trip fee, venue, and the delegate’s kit.



  • WILL YOU PROVIDE THE TRAVEL AND ACCOMMODATION OF THE DELEGATES?

    • The delegates will be the ones responsible to handle and pay for their own travel and accommodation. The FYS Central Committee will be releasing a list of recommended accommodations for the participants.



  • IN CASE I WITHDRAW MY APPLICATION, DO YOU GIVE REFUNDS?

    • Once paid and wish to withdraw your application, the FYS Central Committee is not allowing refunds.



    ABOUT THE SUMMIT

    Frequently Asked Questions

  • HOW SHOULD I PREPARE FOR THE SUMMIT?

    • Everything that you need to know as a delegate is included in the delegate preparation guide. Since you are assigned to a respective pillar, topics that will be talked about will be released beforehand. Thus, you are required to research about the topic and how it affects your province. Make sure to prepare a speech about your province & region’s stand, proposals, and the like.



  • WHAT AWARDS ARE YOU GOING TO GIVE?

    • Aside from the informal/superlative awards to be given in your respective pillar, we are also giving the following awards: Outstanding Delegate/s, Outstanding Policy Paper, and Outstanding Delegation Award.



  • DO YOU GIVE EXCUSED LETTERS TO DELEGATES?

    • Yes, we do give excused letters to delegates who request for one. Should you need one, please give your info to your assigned youth leader.



    OTHERS
